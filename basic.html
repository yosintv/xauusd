The Golden Rule of Gold Candlesticks

Candlestick patterns alone are not trade signals. For XAU/USD, success rate dramatically increases only when the pattern appears at a high-probability context:

  • • Daily / Weekly S/R zones
  • • Previous day high/low
  • • Psychological levels ($1900, $2000, $2100, $2200...)
  • • Fibonacci 50%/61.8%/78.6%
  • • Order blocks / liquidity pools
  • • Major news reaction (NFP, CPI, FOMC)

A Shooting Star in the middle of a range = noise.
A Shooting Star at $2080 (multi-year high) = potential fortune.

Bullish Engulfing

Large bullish candle completely engulfs the body of the previous bearish candle.

Typical context

Support zone • After impulsive leg down • London/NY session open

"$2015 holds → massive green H1 candle closes above $2022 → strong reversal"

Shooting Star

Small body near lows + very long upper shadow (≥2.5–3× body). Strong rejection.

Typical context

Resistance • After parabolic move • News spike failure

"$2080 CPI spike rejected → long wick → distribution begins"

Morning Star

3-candle reversal: strong bear • small indecision (gap down) • strong bull closing >50% into 1st candle.

Typical context

Major low • Exhaustion • Asian range → London reversal

Marubozu

Full-bodied candle with almost no shadows — complete dominance by buyers or sellers.

Do NOT fade Marubozu after news (NFP, rate decision, geopolitics).

Doji Family

Open ≈ Close. Market equilibrium / indecision.

• Dragonfly → bullish reversal potential
• Gravestone → bearish reversal potential
• Long-legged → high volatility

Very common 15–60 min before FOMC / NFP — wait for breakout.

Tweezer Bottom

Two or more candles with matching (or nearly identical) lows — strong support found.

Precision matters

Best when lows match within 0.10–0.30 USD on Gold
