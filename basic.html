Advanced Trading Strategies & Context Rules

‚≠ê Higher Probability Setups

  • Pattern + S/R Zone: Bullish Engulfing at $2000 support = 85% win rate
  • Pattern + News: Marubozu after NFP = strong momentum continuation
  • Multi-timeframe Confirmation: H4 Morning Star + D1 support = high-probability long
  • Liquidity Clusters: Patterns at round numbers ($2050, $2100) are stronger
  • Session Open Patterns: London open (H1 candlestick) more reliable than Asia

‚ùå Avoid These Traps

  • Pattern in the middle of range: Noise. Wait for structure.
  • Doji before major news: Don't trade ‚Äî volatility expansion will happen
  • Reversal pattern vs. strong trend: Hammers fail in downtrends ‚Äî use with caution
  • Trading into news without plan: Spreads widen, slippage increases 3‚Äì5x
  • Ignoring risk/reward: Even 90% win-rate pattern loses money with bad R:R

Risk Management Rules for Gold Candlesticks

1

Stop Loss Placement

Place SL 5‚Äì10 pips beyond the pattern's high/low, not exactly at it. Wicks can trigger stops before reversal.

2

Risk:Reward Minimum 1:2

Never take a trade unless your profit target is at least 2x your risk. Candlestick patterns alone don't guarantee wins.

3

Position Sizing

Risk no more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. Use micro lots or adjust shares accordingly.

4

Avoid Overtrading Near News

30 minutes before/after major economic data, volatility spikes and patterns fail. Skip these windows.

5

Confirm with Volume / Price Action

A pattern is stronger if accompanied by higher volume and follow-through. Wait for next candle confirmation.

Trading Psychology & Common Mistakes

‚ùå Mistake #1: "Perfect" Pattern Hunting

Waiting for the "textbook perfect" Morning Star costs you 10 setups. Real trading patterns are 80% textbook, not 100%. Accept "good enough" patterns at strong structure.

‚ùå Mistake #2: Pattern Chasing

Seeing a pattern that already reversed and trying to enter late. Always enter at pattern completion, not at profit-taking stage.

‚ùå Mistake #3: Revenge Trading

Losing on a Shooting Star short, then immediately taking a Bullish Engulfing long without proper plan. Emotions + patterns = account wipeout.

‚ùå Mistake #4: Ignoring Confluence

Taking an isolated Doji formation without checking daily S/R. The stronger the confluence, the higher the edge. Always zoom out.

How Gold Responds to Major Economic Events

üìä

NFP (Non-Farm Payroll) - 1st Friday of Month

Gold spikes on weak data (usually down), reverses sharply on strong data. Expect Marubozu or Shooting Stars. Volatility often 50+ pips in 10 minutes.

üìä

CPI (Inflation Data) - Monthly

Higher inflation = Gold rallies. Lower inflation = Gold dumps. Watch for reversal patterns at psychological levels post-release.

üìä

FOMC Rate Decision - Every 6 weeks

Doji patterns 1 hour before announcement common. On decision: strong Marubozu (direction depends on hawkish/dovish tone). Don't fight the Marubozu momentum.

üìä

Fed Chair Speech / Geopolitical News

Unpredictable direction, but always creates sharp Marubozu moves. Risk/reward on these event patterns is usually poor unless you predict the direction correctly.

Timeframe Strategies: Which Pattern Works Best Where

M15 / M30 (Scalping)

Best patterns: Hammer, Shooting Star, Doji

Focus on tight stops (3-5 pips), quick profits (10-20 pips). Patterns form faster but fail faster. Requires constant chart watching.

H1 (Swing/Intraday)

Best patterns: Engulfing, Morning Star, Marubozu

Sweet spot for beginners. Clear patterns, reliable S/R zones. Targets 40-100+ pips. Can trade 2-3 times per day.

H4 (Swing/Intermediate)

Best patterns: Morning/Evening Star, 3-day patterns

High accuracy patterns. Targets 100-300+ pips. Trade 1-2 times per week. Requires patience between setups.

D1 (Long-term/Position)

Best patterns: Morning/Evening Star, Engulfing

Highest win rate but slowest. Targets 200-800+ pips. Trade 2-4 times per month. Fits busy schedules.
